Working with me

Strengths
Grit
Absorbs more than most. When people are looking for the exit, looking for the fix. Long hours, hard clients, failing systems — without making it a personality trait.
Insightful
Fast at identifying the actual problem — technical, process, or people. Multiple senior engineers have noted this independently, regardless of experience level.
PM-Conscious
Constantly evaluating the strategic "why" behind every task. Not just executing — thinking about what should be executed and whether the framing is right.
Versatility
Switches between deep technical execution and open strategic thinking without friction. Wide toolkit, all of it gets used.
Fast on Feet
Real-time thinking under pressure: live debugging in front of clients, path-finding when the plan breaks, staying useful when the situation changes.
Communication
Structured, direct, keeps the written record. Pulls others into the conversation. Not a soft skill — a technical advantage on complex projects.
Watch-Outs
Confidence Lull
Independence and output can make it look like oversight isn't needed. It is — everyone needs it. Multiple eyes on big projects matter.
Going at it Alone
Confident that with time and resources anything is achievable. Doesn't mean it'll be done right first, in time, or at the quality the constraints require.
Tunnel Vision
The closer to the critical path, the more narrowly the focus narrows. Stepping back and discussing is necessary overhead, not optional — and it tends to get neglected.
Pushing Past the Signal
Takes more pressure than most. That sometimes means ignoring signals to step back. If you see it coming first, say so directly.
How to Collaborate
Build in the challenge
Confidence and quality output can make it look like due diligence isn't needed. It is. Don't let the results talk you out of your process.
Involve in the full process
Full contribution comes from problem definition through iteration — not just the execution phase. That's where the full skill set gets used.
Bring in early
A thought partner, not a ticket-taker. Define the problem first, then execute — not the reverse.
State your priorities
The clearer the priorities — speed, stability, quality, cost — the better the output. Early correction beats late discovery.
How I'm Working On It
Confidence Lull
Scheduled check-ins, made known to relevant peers. Systems catch what human intuition misses.
Going at it Alone
Scheduled support even when it doesn't feel needed, used religiously. Trusted peers in PM, engineering, and sales as standing references.
Tunnel Vision
A 30-minute step back every 4 hours on deep work — just enough to keep the full system in view.
Pushing Past the Signal
Ruthless self-awareness. Systemic mode-switching. Built into the schedule, not left to willpower.
